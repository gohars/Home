Pakistani Cuisine

You may be familiar with Pakistani cuisine but is it under the term of Indian Cuisine. Most Indian and Pakistani cuisine is very similar due to being from the same general area-South Asia. Most of the food that is going to be shown on this webpage are popular with both Pakistani and Indian people.Pakistani cuisine is known for its richness and flavour. Within Pakistan, cuisine varies greatly from region to region, reflecting the country's ethnic and cultural diversity.

Breakfast

A typical Pakistani breakfast or Nashta (ناشتہ‎) is usually comprised of, but not limited to Chai, Parata and Halwa Puri. Here are many other dishes that Pakistanis eat for breakfast but as I said before, it mainly depends on the region of Pakistan that you are in, the closer the region is to another country the more it inhibits from their culture.

Paratha

Paratha is a flatbread which is made of wheat flour and is shallow fried. There are different kinds of parathas like filled with potatoes, cauliflower, radishes and even cheese. Parathas are eaten as a breakfast dish usually with chai and eggs.

Chai

Chai is usually referred to as tea or chai tea. In Pakistan, Chai is the word for tea so when you say " Can I have some Chai Tea?" you will essentially be saying " Can I have some Tea Tea". Chai in Pakistanis usually mixed with milk. The word chai is used in many parts of the world and refers to tea.It’s usually taken with breakfast and also in the evening with snacks.

Halwa Puri

Halwa Puri is one of the most famous and popular breakfast dishes in Pakistan. Puri is deep fried bread served with halwa. Halwa is a sweet dish made of semolina fried in ghee with sugar and garnished with nuts.

Aloo Bhaji

Aloo Bhaji is a dish of spicy potatoes with gravy.It's usually eaten with Puri or paratha. This can be served for a casual breakfast, lunch or dinner.

Cholay

Cholay is eaten with puri. The main ingredient is chickpeas, cooked with onion, chopped tomatoes, and spices.
