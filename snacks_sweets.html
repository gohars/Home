Snacks And Sweets

In Pakistan, snacks are usually eaten in the evening with a hot cup of Chai and Sweets are usually eaten after Lunch or Dinner. In the month of Ramadan, most Muslims in Pakistan are fasting so when they break their fasts they have alot of snacks and sweets with their main dish. Some of these are an everyday item for those fasting in the month of Ramadan.

Fruit Chaat

Fruit chaat is a dish consisting of various kinds of fruits cut into small cubes and is flavoured with chaat masala (spices) and sugar.

Gulab Jamun

Gulab jamun is a very famous Pakistani dessert. Its soft, fried dumplings traditionally made of thickened or reduced milk, soaked in rose flavored sugar syrup. It will just melt in your mouth! Gulab Jamun looks like Timbits soaked in sweet syrup.

Jalebi

Jalebi is made by deep frying flour batter in circular shape which is then soaked in a sweet Syrup. It is served both warm or cold. Some people also eat jalebi with milk.

Kheer

Kheer is like rice pudding which is made by cooking rice with milk and sugar. It is flavoured with cardamom, raisins, pistachios or almonds. It is typically served during a meal or as a dessert.

Pakora

A good Ramadan snack (especially for vegetarians). Pakora is made up of vegetables and gram flour. They're fried so they have a crunchy texture.

Samosa

Samosas are a very popular snack in Pakistan. These are like little pastries that are filled with either meat or vegetables. Samosas are deep fried. You can also make your own samosas at home by buying samosa pastry sheets and filling them with any of your favourite stuffings, and then deep frying it.
