Lunch and Dinner

For lunch and dinner, Pakistanis eat the same dishes interchangeably. When making lunch we usually prepare for dinner at the same time. Of course, anything that goes with the main dish, like salads, raita, and beverages are made only when the dish is being eaten. On this page, you will learn more about what Pakistanis eat at lunch and dinner.

Biryani

Biryani is a dish comprised of rice and meat. The rice and meat are partially cooked beforehand and then layered together and cooked on low heat. The rice is usually basmati rice and the meat can be lamb, chicken, or fish.

Pulao

Pulao is a rice dish, cooked in seasoned broth with rice, meat, and spices. In Pakistan, Pulao is a popular dish cooked with Basmati rice and meat (beef,mutton,chicken).

Keema

Keema or qeema is a traditional meat dish. Keema is a dish made from minced meat. It is minced mutton curry which is cooked with peas or potatoes.

Roti and Naans

Roti/Naan is flatbread. Roti and naan are staple food made from wheat flour. Pakistani curries are always eaten with either Naan or Roti. Pakistani lunch or dinner are incomplete without roti/naan.
